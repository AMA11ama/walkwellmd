First MTP Joint Fusion – Rehabilitation Protocol

WalkWellMD • Post‑operative patient instructions

Urgent concerns? Fever, worsening pain/swelling, wound redness or discharge, calf pain, shortness of breath, numbness/discoloration of toes.

Follow‑up visits

  • Day 3: wound check
  • Day 9: wound check and X‑rays
  • Day 15: suture removal
  • Then every 2 weeks until full bone healing is confirmed

Early Care

Weeks 0–2

  • Immobilization: Bulky dressing or post‑operative shoe. Do not remove or get wet until your surgeon confirms the wound is fully healed.
  • Elevation: Keep the foot above heart level as much as possible.
  • Weight‑bearing: Walk on your heel only in the post‑op shoe. No weight on the forefoot/big toe. Use crutches as needed.
  • Medication:
    • Antibiotics for 7 days — complete the full course.
    • Pain control: one narcotic + one non‑narcotic.
      • First 72 h: take both regularly.
      • Days 4–6: take the non‑narcotic regularly.
      • From day 7: only as needed.
  • Blood clot prevention: Aspirin 100 mg once daily for 14 days (unless instructed otherwise).
  • Precautions: No driving, sports, barefoot walking, or pressure on the forefoot.

Weeks 2–6

  • Continue heel‑only walking in the post‑op shoe.
  • Dressing changes in clinic only.
  • Sutures usually removed at day 15.
  • Begin gentle ankle and lesser toe motion (not the fused big toe).
  • Continue elevation and ice for swelling.

Weeks 6–8

  • X‑rays to assess bone healing.
  • If early fusion is seen, gradually transition to full weight‑bearing in a stiff‑soled/rocker‑bottom shoe, avoiding excessive push‑off.
  • Start gentle gait training in physiotherapy.
  • Avoid running, jumping, or any activity that bends the big toe.

Weeks 8–12

  • Continue physiotherapy for ankle/forefoot flexibility (excluding the fused joint).
  • Emphasize balance, strength, and normal gait mechanics.
  • Return to desk work and most daily activities.
  • Avoid high heels or tight shoes.

Months 3–6

  • Gradual return to full activity as comfort allows.
  • Most patients resume normal walking and light sports (cycling, swimming) by 3–4 months.
  • Full fusion typically achieved by 4–6 months.

Swelling & Elevation

  • Swelling is normal after surgery and may last for several months.
  • Keep the foot elevated above heart level when resting.
  • Use ice packs for 15–20 minutes at a time, several times daily, especially after activity.
  • Swelling often increases at the end of the day — rest and elevate.
