Partial Nail Bed Excision with Cauterization – Post-Operative Protocol

By WalkWellMD (clinic instruction sheet)

Follow-up Visits

  • Day 3: wound check
  • Day 9: wound check and dressing change
  • Day 15: review and healing assessment
  • Then as needed until full nail bed healing

Early Care (Weeks 0–2)

  • Wound Care:
    • Week 1: Daily dressing changes with a thin layer of topical antibiotic ointment (e.g., Fusidic acid or Mupirocin).
    • Week 2: Change the dressing every third day or sooner if it becomes wet or soiled.
    • Keep the area clean, dry, and covered with sterile gauze.
    • No soaking; do not allow water on the wound until cleared by your doctor.
  • Foot Elevation: Elevate above heart level during the first 48 hours to reduce swelling.
  • Weight-bearing: Walk carefully in a post-op sandal/open-toe shoe, avoiding direct pressure on the operated toe.
  • Medication:
    • Antibiotics (oral) for 7 days – complete the full course.
    • Pain control: first 72 hours take both the stronger (narcotic) and regular (non-narcotic) pain relievers as scheduled; afterward continue the non-narcotic only as needed.
  • Blood clot prevention: Aspirin once daily for 14 days unless instructed otherwise.
  • Precautions: No swimming, soaking, or tight footwear.

Weeks 2–4

  • Once the surface dries and scabs, reduce dressing changes to twice weekly.
  • Continue open-toe shoes/sandals until fully healed.
  • Mild drainage or yellowish crust can be normal while the cauterized area heals.
  • No nail polish or pedicure until approved by your surgeon.

Weeks 4–6

  • Wound should be dry and epithelialized.
  • Gradually resume normal shoes with a wide toe box.
  • Avoid narrow/tight shoes to prevent recurrence.

Months 2–3

  • Nail margin continues to remodel and smoothen.
  • Resume all activities, including sports, once fully healed and pain-free.
Red Flags — call us immediately: Fever > 38°C; persistent or foul-smelling drainage; worsening redness, pain, or swelling; increasing warmth or spreading infection around the toe; numbness or dark discoloration.

Action: Contact our clinic at 0127444444 to schedule an appointment with your doctor as soon as possible, or go to the emergency department.

Swelling & Elevation

  • Mild swelling is expected for 1–2 weeks.
  • Elevate when resting; apply ice (wrapped) for 15 minutes several times daily.
  • Avoid prolonged standing during the first few days.
