Achilles Tendon Rupture — Rehabilitation Program

By WalkWellMD (clinic instruction sheet)

Important: Follow-up as scheduled. If you develop increasing pain, swelling, numbness, fever, calf tenderness, or the boot feels too tight/loose, contact the clinic.

Week 0–2

  • Plantarflexion cast or boot locked in plantarflexion.
  • Non–weight bearing with crutches.

Week 2–4

  • Transition to walker boot with 2–4 cm heel lifts.
  • Protected weight bearing (in boot with lifts) using crutches:
    • Week 2–3: 25% body weight
    • Week 3–4: 50% body weight
    • Week 4–5: 75% body weight
    • Week 5–6: 100% body weight

Week 4–6

  • Active ankle ROM (out of boot when seated, 3–5×/day):
    • Plantarflexion and dorsiflexion to neutral only.
    • Inversion and eversion below neutral.
  • Do not go past neutral in dorsiflexion.

Week 6–8

  • Remain in boot; gradually reduce heel lifts as instructed (e.g., remove 1 lift every 1–2 weeks if pain-free).
  • Progress to full weight bearing in boot with a normal heel-toe pattern.
  • Continue ROM to neutral; begin gentle isometrics (plantarflexion, inversion/eversion) pain-free only.

Week 8–10

  • Wean from boot to a supportive shoe with a small heel lift/gel insert if advised.
  • Begin light resistance band work (plantarflexion, inversion/eversion) without pain.
  • Balance work: double-leg then single-leg stance as tolerated (holding support initially).

Week 10–12

  • Gait normalization in shoes; progress calf strengthening (seated then standing calf raises, starting double-leg → single-leg).
  • Increase functional balance and proprioception (wobble board, soft surface).

After 12 weeks

  • Advance strengthening and endurance; gradual return to low-impact cardio (bike, elliptical, pool running).
  • Higher-impact drills and sport-specific work only with clinician clearance.
Precautions: No forced dorsiflexion past neutral until cleared. No jumping, running, or explosive push-off early in rehab. Use crutches if pain or limp develops.
