Peroneal Tendon Repair – Rehabilitation Protocol

Urgent concerns?

If you experience any of the following, go to the Emergency Department or call us:

  • Fever > 38°C
  • Worsening pain or swelling not relieved by rest/medication
  • Calf pain/swelling or sudden shortness of breath
  • Wound redness, pus, or foul odor
  • Numbness or inability to move toes

Follow‑up Visits

  • Day 3: wound check
  • Day 9: wound check
  • Day 15: suture removal
  • Then every 2 weeks until cast/boot removal

Weeks 0–2 Early Care

  • Immobilization: Splint or cast. Do not remove. Keep dry until the surgeon confirms wound healing.
  • Elevation: Above heart level as much as possible.
  • Weight‑bearing: Non‑weight bearing with crutches/walker.
  • Medication:
    • Antibiotics for 7 days (complete the course).
    • Pain control: stronger + standard pain reliever
      • 0–72 h: both regularly.
      • Days 4–6: non‑narcotic regularly.
      • Day 7+: only if needed.
    • DVT prophylaxis: Aspirin 100 mg once daily for 14 days (unless advised otherwise).
  • Precautions: No driving, sports, or heavy lifting.

Weeks 2–6

  • Transition to Air Cast / CAM boot; wear at all times except bathing/dressing wounds.
  • Light weight only for balance with crutches (not full weight).
  • Gentle toe and ankle motion within boot as instructed.

Weeks 6–8

  • Continue boot; gradually increase weight‑bearing with crutch support.
  • Physiotherapy starts: ankle ROM, edema control (ice, elevation, gentle massage).
  • No running, jumping, or twisting.

Weeks 8–12

  • Discontinue boot when cleared by your surgeon.
  • Full weight‑bearing in supportive shoes.
  • PT focus: strengthening, balance/proprioception, gait correction.

Months 3–6

  • Progress to treadmill walking and light jogging if pain‑free.
  • Delay side‑to‑side sports until cleared.
  • Return to desk work; heavy labor requires clearance.

Months 6+

  • Gradual return to full sport once strength and balance are restored and you are cleared.

Swelling & Elevation

  • Swelling is common and may last months.
  • Elevate above heart level at rest.
  • Ice 15–20 minutes, several times daily.
  • Expect more swelling late in the day — rest and elevate.
