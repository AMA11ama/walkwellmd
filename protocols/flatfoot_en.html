Flatfoot (Pes Planovalgus) Reconstruction — Post-Operative Rehabilitation Protocol

WalkWellMD clinic instruction sheet

Follow-Up Visits

  • Day 3: wound check
  • Day 9: wound check ± X-ray
  • Day 15: suture removal
  • Then every 2 weeks until cast/boot removal and early physiotherapy clearance

Early Care (Weeks 0–2)

  • Immobilization: below-knee back slab or short-leg cast. Keep completely dry and do not remove.
  • Elevation: keep the foot above heart level as much as possible to reduce swelling.
  • Weight-bearing: Non-weight-bearing with crutches or a walker.
  • Medication:
    • Antibiotics for 7 days — complete the full course.
    • Pain control: one stronger (narcotic) + one standard (non-narcotic).
      • First 72 hours → take both regularly.
      • Days 4–6 → take the non-narcotic regularly.
      • From day 7 → take only if needed.
    • Blood-clot prevention: daily aspirin for 14 days unless advised otherwise.
  • Precautions: No driving, no sports, no barefoot walking; keep the cast dry and protected.

Protected Phase (Weeks 2–6)

  • Change to an Air Cast / CAM boot.
  • Heel weight-bearing only as tolerated; use crutches for support. Avoid pressure on forefoot/midfoot.
  • Wound should be dry and healed.
  • Begin gentle, pain-free ankle dorsiflexion/plantarflexion and toe motion exercises out of boot if approved.
  • Continue elevation and 15–20 min icing sessions several times daily.

Progressive Loading (Weeks 6–10)

  • Progress to weight-bearing as tolerated in boot after surgeon clearance and X-ray evidence of healing.
  • Physiotherapy: ankle, subtalar, and midfoot mobility; peroneal and posterior tibial strengthening; gentle gastroc–soleus stretching; balance and proprioception.
  • Continue boot for outdoor activities.

Transition Phase (Weeks 10–14)

  • Transition to a supportive shoe with firm sole and good arch support.
  • Gradually increase walking distance; add treadmill walking and stationary bike.
  • Continue progressive strength and balance rehab.

Return to Activity (3–6 Months)

  • Continue rehab until normal gait is restored.
  • Sport-specific training only after radiographic union is confirmed.
  • Low-impact sports (cycling, swimming) ≈ 3 months; running/impact ≈ 5–6 months.
  • Custom orthotics recommended for 6–12 months to support the arch.
Red Flags — Call Us Immediately: Fever > 38 °C; worsening pain/swelling; wound redness, pus, or foul odor; calf pain or sudden shortness of breath; numbness or toe color change.

Swelling & Elevation

  • Swelling is expected for several months.
  • Elevate when resting; use ice packs 15–20 minutes at a time, especially after activity.
  • Swelling often increases at the end of the day — rest and elevate.
