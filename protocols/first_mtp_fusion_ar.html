First MTP Joint Fusion – Rehabilitation Protocol

By WalkWellMD (clinic instruction sheet)

Follow-up Visits

  • Day 3: wound check
  • Day 9: wound check and X-rays
  • Day 15: suture removal
  • Then every 2 weeks until full bone healing confirmed

Early Care (Weeks 0–2)

  • Immobilization: Bulky dressing or post-operative shoe. Do not remove it and keep it dry (no water on the surgical site until your surgeon confirms full wound healing).
  • Elevation: Foot above heart level as much as possible to reduce swelling.
  • Weight-bearing: Heel-only walking in the post-op shoe. No weight on the forefoot or big toe. Use crutches as needed.
  • Medication:
    • Antibiotics for 7 days – complete the full course.
    • Pain management:
      • First 72 hours: take the narcotic and non-narcotic regularly, regardless of pain.
      • Days 4–6: take the non-narcotic regularly.
      • From day 7 onward: take pain medication only if needed.
  • Blood clot prevention: Aspirin once daily for 14 days unless instructed otherwise.
  • Precautions: No driving, no sports, no barefoot walking, and no pressure on the forefoot.

Weeks 2–6

  • Continue heel-only walking in the post-op shoe.
  • Dressing is changed in clinic; do not remove it yourself.
  • Sutures are usually removed at day 15.
  • Gentle movement of the ankle and the other toes (not the fused big toe).
  • Continue elevation and ice as needed for swelling.

Weeks 6–8

  • X-rays to assess bone healing.
  • If early fusion is seen, gradually transition to full weight-bearing in a stiff-soled or rocker-bottom shoe, avoiding excessive push-off.
  • Begin gentle gait training in physiotherapy to restore a normal pattern.
  • No running, jumping, or activities that bend the big toe.

Weeks 8–12

  • Physiotherapy for ankle/forefoot flexibility (excluding the fused joint).
  • Emphasize balance, strength, and walking mechanics.
  • Return to desk work and most daily activities as comfort allows.
  • Avoid high heels or tight shoes.

Months 3–6

  • Gradual return to full activity.
  • Most patients resume normal walking and light sports (cycling, swimming) by 3–4 months.
  • Full fusion is usually achieved by 4–6 months.
Red Flags — call us immediately: Fever > 38°C; worsening pain or swelling not relieved by rest/medication; wound redness, pus, or foul odor; calf pain/swelling or sudden shortness of breath; numbness or discoloration in the toes.

Action: Contact our clinic at 0127444444 to schedule an appointment with your doctor as soon as possible, or go to the emergency department.

Swelling & Elevation

  • Swelling is normal after surgery and may last for several months.
  • Elevate above heart level when resting.
  • Ice 15–20 minutes several times daily, especially after activity.
  • Swelling often increases at the end of the day — rest and elevate.
