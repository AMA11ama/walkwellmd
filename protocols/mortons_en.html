Morton’s Neuroma Excision – Rehabilitation Protocol

WalkWellMD • Post-operative patient instructions

Urgent concerns? Fever, worsening pain/swelling, wound redness or discharge, calf pain, shortness of breath, numbness or spreading tingling in toes.

Follow-up visits

  • Day 3: wound check
  • Day 9: wound check
  • Day 15: suture removal
  • Then every 2 weeks until fully healed

Early Care

Weeks 0–2

  • Immobilization: Bandage or post-operative shoe. Do not remove it or get it wet until your surgeon confirms complete wound healing.
  • Elevation: Keep the foot above heart level as much as possible.
  • Weight-bearing: Walk carefully in the post-op shoe; avoid full pressure on the ball of the foot.
  • Medication:
    • Antibiotics for 7 days — complete the full course.
    • Pain control: narcotic + non-narcotic.
      • First 72 h: take both regularly.
      • Days 4–6: take the non-narcotic regularly.
      • From day 7: only as needed.
  • Blood clot prevention: Aspirin 100 mg once daily for 14 days (unless instructed otherwise).
  • Precautions: No driving, sports, barefoot walking, or heavy lifting.

Weeks 2–4

  • Continue using the post-op shoe until cleared by your surgeon.
  • Short-distance walking allowed; avoid high-impact activity.
  • Sutures removed around day 15.
  • Begin gentle toe movements after suture removal.

Weeks 4–6

  • Gradually transition to supportive shoes with a wide toe box.
  • Start physiotherapy:
    • Scar massage (once wound fully healed).
    • Gentle toe stretching.
    • Gait training to correct limping.
  • Avoid running, jumping, and narrow shoes.

Weeks 6–10

  • Continue physiotherapy focusing on foot muscle strengthening and balance/proprioception.
  • Increase walking distance as tolerated.
  • Pain should be minimal by this stage.

Months 3+

  • Gradual return to all daily activities.
  • Return to sport when cleared by your surgeon — usually around 3–4 months.
  • Long-term: avoid high heels and tight/narrow shoes to reduce recurrence risk.

Swelling & Elevation

  • Swelling is normal after surgery and may last for several months.
  • Keep the foot elevated above heart level when resting.
  • Use ice packs 15–20 minutes several times daily, especially after activity.
  • Swelling often increases at the end of the day — rest and elevate.
